# South India Logistics Seasonality Calendar

## Core Idea

Seasonality is one of the biggest drivers of logistics cost in South India. Truck demand changes based on agricultural cycles, regional festivals, month-end billing pressure, weather, and construction timing.

For Zippy, seasonality should be treated as a pricing and capacity signal. A route that is cheap in the first week of a normal month can become expensive during harvest, festival dispatch, monsoon disruption, or month-end warehouse congestion.

## Seasonality Summary

| Season / Timing | Month | Truck Demand | Price Trend | Key Commodity Flow |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Harvest - Rabi / Yasangi | Apr-May | Peak | High | Paddy, turmeric, cotton |
| Summer | Mar-May | High | Moderate | Mangoes, cement, steel |
| Festivals | Aug-Nov; Jan | Very high in selected sectors | High for reefers and consumer goods | White goods, sweets, textiles, perishables |
| Harvest - Kharif / Samba | Oct-Jan | Peak | Very high | Chillies, paddy, groundnut |
| Rain / monsoon | Jun-Sep; Oct-Dec | Mixed | Variable with risk premium | Essentials up, construction down |
| Month end | Last 3-4 days | Peak | 10-15% premium plus waiting fees | FMCG, FMCD, retail stock push |
| Month start | First 5 days | Normal | Low / stable | General restocking |

## Harvest Season

**Pattern:** Price spike period.

Harvest season is the most volatile logistics period. Truck demand explodes in agricultural belts, while effective supply falls because vehicles get locked in rural pickup points, mandis, mills, and unloading queues.

**Why rates rise:**
- Trucks enter deep rural areas and cannot exit quickly.
- Market-yard loading and unloading is slow.
- A truck that usually completes four short cycles may complete only two.
- Brokers hold capacity during crop arrival peaks.

**Examples:**
- [[Guntur]] / [[Khammam]] chillies: rates to Hyderabad can jump 40-50% during peak harvest.
- [[Thanjavur]] delta paddy: trucks to [[Karur]] and [[Erode]] mills become expensive during Samba harvest.
- [[Nizamabad]] -> [[Hyderabad]] paddy: strong Yasangi and Kharif surge behavior.

**Zippy implication:** Build 15-day pre-booking alerts for harvest lanes and expose mandi wait-time risk in pricing.

## Month-End Billing Pressure

**Pattern:** Warehouse and dispatch congestion.

The last 3-4 days of the month create artificial truck demand because companies push stock to meet sales, billing, and distributor targets.

**Why rates rise:**
- FMCG, FMCD, and retail companies push warehouse dispatches.
- Distributors want stock before month-end invoicing.
- Warehouse gates in [[Chennai]], [[Sankagiri]], [[Bangalore]], and [[Hyderabad]] become congested.
- Trucks wait 24-48 hours at loading gates and charge waiting premiums.

**Price impact:** Usually 10-15% premium, plus waiting fees.

**Zippy implication:** Add month-end congestion scoring for warehouse clusters and recommend early booking or first-week dispatch where possible.

## Festival Season

**Pattern:** Gifting, consumption, and specialized-vehicle pressure.

South India's festival logistics is strongest around Onam, Dussehra, Diwali, and Pongal.

**High-demand flows:**
- White goods from [[Chennai]] / [[Hyderabad]] to [[Tiruppur]], [[Coimbatore]], and Kerala.
- Sweets, ghee, chocolates, and perishables from [[Hyderabad]] to [[Chennai]] / [[Bangalore]].
- Textiles and garments into Kerala, Tamil Nadu interiors, and metro retail.
- Poultry and food movement into Kerala during Onam and holiday periods.

**Supply issue:**
- Reefer trucks become scarce during food-heavy festivals.
- Dry trucks are usually more available, but loading slots become congested.

**Zippy implication:** Create festival commodity calendars and reserve reefer/insulated supply earlier than normal dry goods capacity.

## Rain And Monsoon Season

**Pattern:** Chokepoints, risk premiums, and uneven demand.

Rain reduces some freight demand but increases risk and delay for essential goods.

**Regional timing:**
- South-West Monsoon: June-September, strongest for Kerala and Karnataka.
- North-East Monsoon: October-December, important for Tamil Nadu and coastal Andhra.

**Demand changes:**
- Construction cargo such as cement, steel, sand, and aggregates may fall during heavy rain.
- Food and essential cargo may spike when floods or supply disruption are expected.
- Kerala routes through the Western Ghats face delays, landslides, and transit-time uncertainty.
- Guntur/Nellore flooding can interrupt chilli, rice, and food movement toward Chennai and Telangana.

**Zippy implication:** Add route risk premiums and weather-delay warnings, especially for Coimbatore-Kerala, coastal Andhra, Chennai, and Western Ghats corridors.

## Summer Season

**Pattern:** Mango, power, and pre-monsoon construction period.

Summer is usually operationally easier because weather delays are lower, but demand remains high for agriculture and construction.

**High-demand flows:**
- Mangoes from [[Chittoor]], [[Krishnagiri]], and Chennai-side belts to [[Bangalore]] and [[Chennai]].
- Cement from [[Ariyalur]] and construction materials into metro and Tier-2 projects.
- Steel from [[Salem]] and [[Vizag]] into construction markets.

**Supply behavior:**
- Truck supply is relatively stable because there are fewer weather disruptions.
- Open-body trucks can become tight where mango and construction peaks overlap.

**Zippy implication:** Treat summer as the safest period for heavy bulky industrial movement, but monitor open-truck capacity around mango belts.

## Beginning Of Month Restocking

**Pattern:** Cheapest and most stable booking window.

The first five days of the month are often calmer because distributors have cleared previous dues, fresh orders are planned, and truckers want to start the month with loaded trips.

**Common flows:**
- General restocking into [[Madurai]], [[Belagavi]], [[Warangal]], [[Erode]], and smaller Tier-2/3 markets.
- FMCG and retail replenishment.
- Dealer stock movement after month-end billing cycles reset.

**Price impact:** Usually low or stable; good time to negotiate.

**Zippy implication:** Recommend first-week booking for non-urgent general cargo and planned distribution.

## Operating Calendar

| Period | Risk Level | Main Risk | Booking Advice |
|---|---|---|---|
| First 5 days of month | Low | Normal restocking | Best time for planned low-cost shipments |
| Last 3-4 days of month | Medium-high | Warehouse gate congestion | Book early and price waiting time |
| Mar-May | Medium | Mango plus construction capacity | Good for industrial cargo; watch open trucks |
| Apr-May | High | Rabi/Yasangi harvest | Pre-book agri trucks |
| Jun-Sep | Medium-high | SW monsoon and Kerala/Karnataka delays | Add risk buffer and weather checks |
| Aug-Nov | High | Festival consumption and reefer shortage | Reserve specialized vehicles early |
| Oct-Jan | Very high | Kharif/Samba harvest plus NE monsoon | Avoid last-minute bookings |
| Nov-Dec | Variable | Floods, construction slowdown, essentials spike | Use route-level weather pricing |

## Zippy Product Implications

1. Add a seasonality score to route pricing.
2. Combine harvest calendars, month-end timing, festival demand, and weather risk into truck availability forecasts.
3. Show users why a price changed: harvest lock-in, gate congestion, festival reefer shortage, monsoon risk, or deadhead.
4. Recommend cheaper booking windows for non-urgent freight.
5. Offer pre-booking workflows for harvest and festival corridors.
6. Track warehouse wait times in Chennai, Sankagiri, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Coimbatore, and Vijayawada.
7. Build route-specific monsoon delay buffers for Kerala, Western Ghats, coastal Andhra, and Tamil Nadu NE monsoon corridors.
