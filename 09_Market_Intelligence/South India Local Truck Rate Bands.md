# South India Local Truck Rate Bands

## Purpose

Capture practical market-rate bands for South India so pricing logic has a benchmark layer alongside deterministic cost and dynamic adjustment logic.

## Indicative 2025 Rate Bands

| Vehicle Type | Capacity Pattern | Indicative Range | Typical Use |
|--------------|------------------|------------------|-------------|
| Mini truck or pickup | 0.5-2 tons | Rs 10-25 per km | Last-mile, short city, rural feeder moves |
| LCV | 2-7 tons | Rs 15-40 per km | Intra-city and short-to-mid regional moves |
| Medium truck | 7-12 tons | Rs 20-30 per km | Warehouse and MSME distribution runs |
| 10-wheeler | 16-20 tons | Rs 25-40 per km | Bulk city-to-city and rural supply |
| Multi-axle or container | 20-40 tons | Rs 35-85 per km | Long-haul industrial and bulk corridors |

## Common Quote Add-Ons

| Cost Element | Typical Pattern |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Loading or unloading | Often charged separately by stop or by labor intensity |
| Tolls and permits | Either included transparently or billed separately |
| Driver allowance | More visible on overnight and long-haul jobs |
| Seasonal surcharge | Often appears during festivals, harvest, or diesel spikes |
| Return-load discount | Used to reduce deadhead and protect utilization |

## How To Use These Bands

- Treat them as benchmark guardrails, not as the pricing engine itself.
- Use them to sanity-check quotes generated by [[Distance Based Pricing]] and [[Dynamic Pricing Logic]].
- Expect corridor-level volatility to be sharper in Tier-2 and Tier-3 lanes than on dense metro corridors.
- Preserve tolls, surge, and discount logic as explainable layers rather than hiding them inside one flat per-km number.

## Warnings

- These ranges are indicative and can drift with diesel, demand pulses, monsoon disruption, and local operator scarcity.
- Contract pricing can sit below spot pricing when volume and predictability are strong.
- Spot or urgent pricing can rise materially above these ranges during short-lived supply shocks.
