# GST for Logistics

## Overview

Goods and Services Tax (GST) revolutionized Indian logistics taxation, replacing multiple state taxes with a unified system.

## Key GST Provisions for Logistics

### GST Rates on Transport Services

| Service Type | GST Rate |
|-------------|----------|
| Road freight (B2B) | 5% (with ITC) |
| Road freight (B2C) | 12% |
| Courier services | 18% |
| Warehousing | 18% |

### Input Tax Credit (ITC)

Transport companies can claim ITC on:
- Fuel purchases
- Vehicle maintenance
- Office supplies
- Business travel

## E-way Bill Requirements

| Criteria | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Value > ₹50,000 | Mandatory |
| Interstate | Always required |
| Intrastate | State-dependent |
| Validity | Based on distance |

## Compliance Requirements

### For Transporters
- GST registration in each state (if threshold exceeded)
- Vehicle-wise GSTIN tagging
- E-way bill generation
- GSTR-1, GSTR-3B filing
- Invoice standardization

### Document Requirements
- GST invoice with supplier/receiver GSTIN
- E-way bill number on transport documents
- HSN code for goods
- Place of supply details

## GST Impact on Pricing

| Component | Pre-GST | Post-GST |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| State entry tax | 2-5% | Eliminated |
| CST | 2-12% | Eliminated |
| Octroi | 1-2% | Eliminated |
| Effective logistics tax | 8-15% | 5-12% |

## Challenges

- Multiple state registrations
- ITC matching delays
- E-way bill system downtime
- Compliance complexity for small operators
