# Customer Terms & Privacy Policy Framework

## Purpose

Define the customer-facing policy structure that should govern booking, payment, cancellation, POD, data use, and privacy communication across the platform.

## Why It Matters

- Policy wording shapes trust, dispute outcomes, and operational clarity.
- Customer-facing terms should mirror actual system behavior, not generic legal boilerplate.
- Privacy policy should explain how service delivery, payments, location, and support interactions use personal data.

## Terms and Conditions Scope

| Topic | Why It Must Be Clear |
|------|-----------------------|
| Booking confirmation | Defines when an order is actually confirmed |
| Payment modes | Explains prepaid, part payment, and ToPay conditions |
| Pricing and surcharges | Prevents hidden-fee disputes |
| Cancellation and refunds | Sets expectations around assignment, movement, and wait-time deductions |
| Driver no-show handling | Clarifies replacement, cancellation, and credit outcomes |
| Customer responsibilities | Reduces avoidable disputes around packing, legality, and availability |
| Prohibited goods | Protects legal and safety boundaries |
| POD and delivery proof | Defines when delivery is treated as complete |

## Privacy Policy Scope

| Topic | Why It Must Be Clear |
|------|-----------------------|
| Data collected | Explains what customer, consignee, device, and location data is gathered |
| Purpose of processing | Connects data use to booking, routing, payment, support, and fraud prevention |
| Sharing rules | Limits disclosure to drivers, transport companies, payment providers, and service partners as needed |
| Retention | Sets expectations for orders, GPS logs, and support evidence |
| User rights | Clarifies access, correction, deletion, and consent withdrawal paths |
| Security practices | Builds trust around encryption, access control, and payment handling |

## Operational Alignment Rules

- Booking terms should match the actual confirmation rules in the order lifecycle.
- ToPay terms should align with explicit consent handling and timeout behavior.
- Cancellation policy should match state-aware refund and fee logic.
- POD wording should align with OTP, signature, photo, and alternate-verification workflows.
- Privacy wording should reflect real sharing with assigned providers and payment partners only.

## Important Boundary

- This note supports policy design and product alignment, but final publishable language should still pass legal review.
