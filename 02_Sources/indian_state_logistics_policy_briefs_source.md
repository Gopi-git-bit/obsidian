## Cross-State Pattern

Most state policies converge around the same strategic direction:

```text
PM GatiShakti alignment
+ logistics industry status
+ multimodal infrastructure
+ MMLP / ICD / CFS / logistics park development
+ warehousing and cold-chain incentives
+ single-window clearance
+ digital tracking and command systems
+ sustainability / green logistics
+ skill development
+ city logistics and truck terminal planning
```

The practical startup implication is that warehouses, logistics parks, ICDs, CFSs, cold-chain facilities, and industrial clusters are becoming policy-supported demand nodes. The platform should therefore map state policy incentives directly to customer acquisition, warehouse onboarding, vehicle supply strategy, and corridor prioritization.

## GTM Implications For Zippy

- In Tamil Nadu, lead with warehouse-to-transporter matching, industrial lane intelligence, dock scheduling, and port-linked documentation.
- In Karnataka, lead with Bengaluru warehouse visibility, air-cargo support, cold-chain/pharma/ecommerce dispatch, and city freight intelligence.
- In Telangana, lead with warehouse/cold storage onboarding, district logistics park dispatch workflows, fleet registration/permit awareness, and rice/pharma movement.
- In Andhra Pradesh, lead with port-to-warehouse execution, truck parking/gate-slot coordination, seafood/aqua cold chain, and FTWZ/customs workflows.
- In Gujarat and Maharashtra, sell more mature modules: ICD/CFS, bonded warehouse, smart logistics, EV/green reporting, high-value industrial cluster service.
- In Kerala and Sikkim, avoid generic truck marketplace positioning; sell specialized constrained-geography logistics: small-vehicle routing, cold chain, waterways/air contingency, disaster resilience.
- In Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh, build bulk, agri, mineral, textile, pharma, rail-linked and return-load optimization playbooks before broad marketplace expansion.
