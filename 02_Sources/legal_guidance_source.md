# Legal Guidance Source

## Overview

This source contributes practical guidance around:

- company setup and baseline registrations for logistics operations in India
- customer and MSME contract expectations
- shipment documentation and tax compliance
- hazardous-goods screening and special-handling controls
- compliance-linked trust building for logistics partnerships

## Important Boundary

- This material is useful as operating guidance, not as final legal advice.
- Vault notes derived from it should be treated as policy and compliance framing that still needs validation against current law, contracts, and counsel review.

## Core Takeaways

### 1. Reliability Is Partly a Legal and Contract Design Problem

- MSMEs do not buy only freight movement; they buy predictability, visibility, and recourse.
- Clear contracts, SLAs, compensation rules, and fallback responsibilities are part of service design.

### 2. Documentation Is a Core Control Surface

- POD, invoices, shipment papers, tax records, and compliance documents are not admin overhead.
- They are the evidence base for disputes, tax, and regulatory protection.

### 3. Hazardous-Goods Filtering Should Start Early

- Screening dangerous or restricted cargo at booking is safer than trying to recover later.
- HSN, UN, permit, and MSDS logic belongs in product and compliance workflows.
