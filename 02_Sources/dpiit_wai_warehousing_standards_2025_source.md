# DPIIT-WAI e-Handbook on Warehousing Standards 2025 Source

## Source Identity

The provided source files describe the `e-Handbook on Warehousing Standards (2025 Edition)`.

The handbook is presented as a collaborative initiative led by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India, in partnership with the Warehousing Association of India (WAI).

It is aligned with national logistics modernization priorities including the National Logistics Policy and PM GatiShakti.

## Standards Basis

The handbook consolidates and references standards and practices from:

- Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)
- Warehousing Development and Regulatory Authority (WDRA)
- Central Motor Vehicles Rules (CMVR)
- Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH) truck body/cab/trailer construction practice
- National Building Code and fire safety references
- FSSAI, FCI, National Centre for Cold Chain Development, CPCB, ISO, API, and other domain-specific bodies where applicable

## Applicability

The handbook primarily applies to:

- General-purpose industrial and commercial warehouses
- Warehouses in integrated logistics parks and distribution centres
- Modern high-specification warehouses commonly called Grade A or Grade B in industry usage
- E-commerce, FMCG, and retail fulfilment centres

It references selected standards for:

- Commodity and agricultural warehouses governed by WDRA or BIS guidelines
- Temperature-controlled warehouses
- Underground tanks and open yards

Important limitation: the handbook does not provide detailed design or operational guidance for every specialized warehouse type. Specialized facilities still need their own domain-specific technical codes and regulatory frameworks.

## Grade A/B/C Clarification

The source makes an important distinction:

- Grade A and Grade B are useful commercial/industry terms.
- They are not formal BIS or WDRA classification frameworks.
- No formal Indian standard defines Grade A/B/C as legal classes.
- The distinction is based on functional specifications, safety compliance, infrastructure quality, technology readiness, and operational capability.

Typical Grade A traits in the source include:

- Pre-engineered building structure
- Clear height around 10-12+ metres
- FM2 category flooring with high load-bearing capacity
- Dock levelers and dock-to-door planning
- Fire safety systems such as sprinklers and hydrants as per applicable norms
- Wide column spacing
- Truck parking and turning radius
- Office/admin blocks
- WMS readiness
- Green building readiness in some cases

## Transportation Standards Extract

Transportation is framed as the link between suppliers, warehouses, distribution centres, and customers.

Key objectives:

- Cost efficiency through standardized vehicle and pallet utilization
- Safety and compliance for goods movement
- Operational streamlining across warehousing and transportation
- Risk mitigation for damage, loss, and inefficiency
- Space utilization across trucks and warehouses
- Scalability and flexibility during demand fluctuation
- Sustainability through route optimization, fuel reduction, and vehicle utilization

## Road Transport And Truck Body Standards

The source gives important road transport requirements:

- Road transport infrastructure must adhere to applicable CMVR rules.
- Truck bodies should align with MoRTH code of practice for truck cabs, truck bodies, and trailers.
- Ideal clear internal truck body width: `2286 mm`.
- Width is designed to support common pallet sizes:
  - `1200 mm x 1200 mm`
  - `1200 mm x 1000 mm`
  - `1200 mm x 800 mm`
  - `1140 mm x 1140 mm`
- Include about `50 mm` clearance on either side for pallet fit.
- At least one side should support collapsible flap/side loading where forklift loading is required.
- Rear loading should support forklift/dock-leveler entry where side loading is not used.
- Truck/trailer flooring should be smooth and robust enough for BOPT/forklift movement and pallet stacking.

## Network Optimization Extract

Network design should use:

- Customer demand patterns
- Warehouse locations
- Transportation infrastructure quality
- Delivery lead-time constraints, including immediate to 72-hour delivery models
- Cost-service tradeoff analysis
- Outsourcing/shared logistics where professional providers reduce cost
- Collaboration, including consolidation of freight and committed return loads

## Vehicle Gate Checklist Principle

The source recommends a standardized daily checklist for vehicles entering and leaving the warehouse.

Purpose:

- Improve safety and security
- Protect cargo
- Streamline operations
- Optimize maintenance costs
- Identify issues before they become accidents, delays, or disputes

## Product-Specific Standards

Products stored in warehouses should follow relevant standards for:

- Grading
- Sampling
- Testing
- Weighment

For agricultural products, WDRA/AGMARK and commodity-specific guidelines matter.

For non-agricultural products, standards may include BIS and international bodies such as LME, LBMA, and CME depending on commodity type.

Weighment references include:

- BIS weighbridge specification `IS 1436`
- BIS general weighing instrument requirement `IS 1432`
