# Revenue Model Decision Framework

## Purpose

Provide a structured way to choose and evolve revenue models when market conditions, customer behavior, and operating constraints are still uncertain.

## Core Decision Axes

| Axis | Strategic Question |
|------|--------------------|
| Asset intensity | Should the company own vehicles or orchestrate partner capacity |
| Service depth | Is value created by transport only, or by bundling finance, visibility, warehousing, or compliance |
| Monetization style | Should revenue come from commissions, flat fees, subscriptions, savings share, or mixed models |
| Control versus scalability | How much operational control is needed relative to speed of expansion |
| Segment fit | Which customer group has the strongest pain, willingness to pay, and repeat behavior |

## Common Revenue Models

| Model | Strength | Main Risk |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Asset-based transport | Strong control and reliability | High capital burden and low flexibility |
| Brokerage or commission | Fast to launch and asset-light | Margin pressure and weak moat |
| Bundled 3PL services | More wallet share and stickiness | Operational complexity |
| 4PL or orchestration | Strong strategic position and neutral coordination value | Requires trust, data, and execution discipline |
| SaaS or platform subscription | Predictable recurring revenue | Harder to prove value early |
| Fintech-integrated model | Deep retention through cash-flow utility | Credit, compliance, and risk complexity |
| Performance-based or savings-share | Strong alignment with customer value | Hard measurement and longer sales cycles |

## Zippy-Fit Strategic Reading

- Early-stage advantage is strongest in asset-light orchestration with strong operational intelligence.
- Embedded finance and faster settlement can become a moat where local supply is fragmented.
- Backhaul and empty-mile reduction create monetizable value beyond simple freight commission.
- First-mile cluster operations can be a strong wedge when density is high and shipper behavior is chaotic.
- Credit enablement should stay partner-led so Zippy captures conversion and retention value without taking direct lending risk.

## Decision Under Uncertainty

| Situation | Better Starting Bias |
|----------|----------------------|
| Demand is fragmented and supply is fragmented | Marketplace or orchestration model |
| Reliability is more valuable than price | Bundled service or managed-operations layer |
| Customer cash flow is the main blocker | Fintech-linked or credit-aware model |
| Market trust is low | Transparent flat fee or explainable commission model |
| Segment is high-frequency but low-volume | Subscription plus elastic usage or aggregation model |
| Capital is constrained | Avoid asset-heavy expansion unless utilization is very predictable |

## Strategic Filters

1. Does this model increase retention, not just initial revenue.
2. Does it improve margin through data or automation, not just markup.
3. Can it scale regionally without heavy fixed-cost lock-in.
4. Does it fit the strongest target segment today, not a hypothetical future segment.
5. Can it be tested in one corridor or one customer cluster before broad rollout.

## Zippy Candidate Path

| Phase | Likely Best Fit |
|------|-----------------|
| Early corridor buildout | Commission plus flat fee with strong operational control |
| Marketplace strengthening | Dynamic matching fee plus backhaul monetization |
| Sticky customer expansion | Subscription or managed-service layer for visibility and workflow |
| Supply-side loyalty | Faster settlement and fintech-linked retention features |
| Mature optimization stage | Performance-based pricing tied to savings or SLA outcomes |
