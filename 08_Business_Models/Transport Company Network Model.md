# Transport Company Network Model

## Overview

A network-based operating model where the platform combines its own execution capability with verified partner transport companies to expand capacity, geographic reach, and service reliability without owning all fleet capacity directly.

## Why It Exists

- Own fleet capacity is limited
- Demand varies by corridor and season
- Partner networks reduce empty running and missed orders
- Regional specialists improve execution in unfamiliar markets

## Value Proposition

| For The Platform | For Partner Transporters |
|------------------|--------------------------|
| Flexible capacity expansion | More consistent load flow |
| Better corridor coverage | Reduced deadhead |
| Lower capital intensity | Access to platform demand |
| Faster failure recovery | Better utilization and visibility |

## Core Mechanics

1. Build a verified partner base by corridor and vehicle type
2. Score partners on service quality, responsiveness, and compliance
3. Use own fleet first where efficient
4. Use partner capacity when demand exceeds own capacity or local coverage is weak
5. Monitor performance and rebalance future allocation

## Operating Components

| Component | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| Partner onboarding | Vet capability and compliance |
| Capacity map | Know which partners are available by corridor |
| Assignment rules | Decide when own fleet vs partner fleet is used |
| Communication layer | Keep customer and partner aligned |
| Performance review | Improve network quality over time |

## Economics

| Driver | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Lower capex | Platform avoids buying every vehicle needed |
| Better fill rate | More orders can be accepted |
| Partner fee model | Supports flat-fee or managed margin structures |
| Utilization gain | Better use of both own and partner fleet |

## Key Risks

- Partner service inconsistency
- Weak communication during disruption
- Margin leakage from poorly negotiated assignments
- Customer trust damage if partner quality is hidden or unmanaged
