# CRM and Customer Retention Playbook for Zippy Logistics

## Purpose

This note reframes the CRM source document for the Zippy Logistics operating model.

In logistics, customer relationship management is not only polite communication.

It is an operating system for:

- understanding customer operations
- preventing service failures before complaints appear
- communicating shipment truth before the customer asks
- storing customer knowledge
- improving service after every shipment
- measuring retention and satisfaction with discipline

The CRM principle for Zippy:

```text
know the customer deeply
communicate early
solve fast
prove value
learn continuously
```

## Strategic CRM Thesis

Zippy's customer relationship advantage should come from three connected capabilities:

| Capability | Meaning For Zippy |
|---|---|
| Customer orientation | understand each shipper's route, cargo, urgency, payment cycle, and pain points |
| Technology-based CRM | use apps, dashboards, alerts, templates, and data records to communicate faster |
| Knowledge management | convert customer data, complaints, and shipment outcomes into better future service |

Technology alone is not enough.

The system must capture customer knowledge, organize it, reuse it, and turn it into improved logistics decisions.

## Customer Relationship Model

For every important shipper, Zippy should know:

- who they are
- what they ship
- where they ship
- how often they ship
- which routes hurt them
- what service they value most
- how they approve payments
- where delays usually happen
- who makes operational decisions
- who makes commercial decisions
- what would make them leave

The relationship is healthy only when this knowledge is current and used.

## Customer Profile Standard

Create a profile for every shipper.

Minimum fields:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| company name | account identity |
| industry | service and cargo context |
| main products | handling and vehicle fit |
| regular routes | lane intelligence |
| shipment frequency | retention and planning signal |
| preferred vehicle type | matching accuracy |
| loading time | pickup planning |
| unloading time | delivery planning |
| payment cycle | finance and credit policy |
| delay tolerance | SLA and service-tier fit |
| main pain points | relationship and improvement focus |
| decision maker | renewal and escalation |
| operations contact | daily coordination |
| billing contact | invoice and payment closure |
| complaint history | risk and improvement memory |
| satisfaction score | relationship health |
| next follow-up date | retention discipline |

Customer profile completeness should become a KPI.

## Relationship Ownership

Every customer needs an owner.

| Customer Type | Owner Model |
|---|---|
| small customer | shared account executive or ops owner |
| medium repeat customer | relationship manager |
| high-volume customer | dedicated account manager |
| strategic customer | founder or senior leadership involvement |

The relationship owner is accountable for:

- regular communication
- expectation setting
- complaint closure
- monthly performance review
- payment coordination
- service improvement suggestions
- retention risk monitoring

Rule:

```text
if everyone owns the customer, no one owns the customer
```

## Promise Discipline

Trust is built when Zippy does what it promised, when it promised it.

Track every promise:

| Promise | Compare Against |
|---|---|
| promised pickup time | actual pickup time |
| promised delivery time | actual delivery time |
| promised rate | final invoice |
| promised vehicle | actual vehicle |
| promised update frequency | actual updates sent |
| promised POD timing | actual POD timing |

If a promise breaks, the customer should hear it from Zippy before they need to chase.

Silence creates relationship damage even when the shipment eventually succeeds.

## Customer Engagement Rhythm

Use different communication rhythms by customer type.

| Customer Type | Engagement Rhythm |
|---|---|
| new customer | frequent updates for first 3 shipments |
| regular customer | weekly shipment review |
| high-volume customer | twice-weekly planning call or update |
| strategic customer | monthly business review |
| problem customer | exception review after every issue |
| at-risk customer | recovery call plus 7-day corrective action |

Engagement is not random calling.

It is a designed relationship rhythm.

## Customer Service Quality Drivers

Customer satisfaction in logistics comes from the gap between what was expected and what was experienced.

Control these five areas.

## 1. Reliability

Customer sees reliability through:

- on-time pickup
- on-time delivery
- correct vehicle
- correct documents
- damage-free delivery
- POD on time

## 2. Visibility

Customer sees visibility through:

- live status
- proactive delay alerts
- ETA updates
- digital POD
- clear escalation contact

## 3. Responsiveness

Customer sees responsiveness through:

- first response time
- fast issue resolution
- clear ownership
- no repeated chasing

## 4. Cost Fairness

Customer sees cost fairness through:

- transparent pricing
- no surprise charges
- clear detention policy
- accurate billing
- pre-confirmed rate sheet

## 5. Customer-Specific Fit

Customer sees fit through:

- service matching their industry
- vehicle matching their cargo
- update rhythm matching their urgency
- payment process matching their business reality
- route plan matching their lane pattern

## Complaint Management SOP

A complaint is customer research with urgency.

Complaint categories:

- late pickup
- late delivery
- no update
- wrong vehicle
- high rate
- damage or loss
- POD delay
- billing issue
- driver behavior
- loading delay
- unloading delay
- invoice or payment confusion

Handling steps:

1. Acknowledge within 15 minutes.
2. Assign an owner.
3. Identify root cause.
4. Tell the customer the next update time.
5. Solve or contain the issue.
6. Confirm customer satisfaction.
7. Record cause and category.
8. Create prevention action.

Root-cause examples:

| Complaint | Possible Root Cause | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| late pickup | vehicle assigned too late | assignment deadline before pickup |
| no update | tracking process missing | scheduled update workflow |
| POD delay | driver not trained | digital POD checklist |
| billing dispute | rate unclear | pre-confirmed rate sheet |
| damage | poor handling or packaging | handling SOP and partner audit |
| loading delay | material not ready | pickup readiness confirmation |

## Retention Playbook

## New Customers

Goal:

```text
build trust fast
```

Actions:

- explain process clearly
- confirm expectations before first shipment
- give frequent updates
- ask feedback after first shipment
- fix first problems quickly
- capture customer profile fully

## Regular Customers

Goal:

```text
become easier to keep than replace
```

Actions:

- give priority service where justified
- offer route improvement ideas
- share monthly performance reports
- monitor satisfaction trend
- suggest cost-saving and delay-reduction actions
- maintain payment and billing clarity

## At-Risk Customers

Risk signals:

- shipment frequency drops
- complaints increase
- payment delays increase
- customer stops replying
- customer asks repeatedly for old invoices
- customer compares rates aggressively
- decision maker becomes unavailable

Recovery actions:

- call decision maker
- share performance report
- ask what changed
- identify service gap
- create corrective plan
- deliver one visible improvement within 7 days
- schedule follow-up review

## Strategic Customers

Goal:

```text
co-design logistics improvement
```

Actions:

- dedicated account owner
- quarterly business review
- custom dashboard
- joint route planning
- contract pricing
- service-level agreement
- carrier pool alignment
- payment and invoice discipline

## Monthly Customer Performance Report

Send repeat customers a simple proof-of-value report.

Include:

- total shipments handled
- on-time pickup percentage
- on-time delivery percentage
- average loading delay
- average unloading delay
- POD submission time
- claims or damage cases
- billing disputes
- empty km reduced
- estimated savings
- top complaint reasons
- next improvement suggestions

This report turns service into visible value.

Without proof, Zippy risks being compared only on freight rate.

## Customer Survey Template

Use after shipment for new customers and periodically for regular customers.

Ask from 1 to 5:

1. Vehicle arrived on time.
2. Delivery was completed as promised.
3. Shipment updates were clear and timely.
4. Zippy responded quickly to questions.
5. Documentation and POD process was smooth.
6. Pricing and billing were clear.
7. The overall experience was easy.
8. Overall, how satisfied are you with this shipment?
9. How likely are you to use Zippy again?
10. What should Zippy improve next time?

NPS question:

```text
on a scale of 0 to 10, how likely are you to recommend Zippy to another business?
```

CES question:

```text
how easy was it to complete this shipment with Zippy?
```

## Information Flow Across Order Lifecycle

CRM must connect to the order lifecycle.

```text
inquiry
-> quotation
-> confirmation
-> vehicle/load planning
-> pickup scheduling
-> loading
-> in-transit tracking
-> delivery
-> POD
-> billing
-> payment
-> feedback
```

At each stage, the customer relationship can improve or weaken.

Critical information by stage:

| Stage | CRM-Critical Information |
|---|---|
| inquiry | origin, destination, load, vehicle, pickup, deadline |
| quotation | rate, assumptions, return-load possibility, payment terms |
| confirmation | contacts, slots, documents, cancellation terms |
| planning | driver, vehicle, carrier quality, route restrictions |
| pickup | gate rule, loading bay, material readiness |
| loading | quantity, condition, seal, departure time |
| transit | GPS, ETA, delay alert, next update time |
| delivery | arrival, unloading, receiver, damage/shortage notes |
| POD | signed proof, photo, timestamp, GPS, remarks |
| billing | final rate, detention, tax, due date, dispute status |
| feedback | satisfaction, complaint reason, improvement idea |

## Customer Retention Rules

1. Do not let important customers chase for basic shipment status.
2. Do not leave complaints uncategorized.
3. Do not close a complaint until customer satisfaction is confirmed.
4. Do not rely only on operational success; measure customer perception.
5. Do not wait for churn; detect frequency drop early.
6. Do not sell only freight rate; prove route, visibility, and reliability value.
7. Do not build customer knowledge unless it is reused in future service.
