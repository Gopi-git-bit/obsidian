# SOP - Customer Communication

## Trigger

Any customer-facing interaction that requires a structured status update, expectation reset, issue explanation, or post-action follow-up.

## Preconditions

- Customer identity or order reference is available
- Latest operational status has been checked
- The communication owner is clear

## Steps

### Step 1: Confirm Context

```text
1.1 Check order ID, customer name, and active issue
1.2 Verify the latest status from system or operations team
1.3 Identify what the customer actually needs right now
1.4 Choose the correct channel for the update
```

### Step 2: Prepare The Message

```text
2.1 State the current situation clearly
2.2 Explain the impact in customer terms
2.3 Share next action and expected timeline
2.4 Avoid assumptions or unverified promises
```

### Step 3: Deliver The Update

```text
3.1 Send the update through the primary channel
3.2 Use simple, direct language
3.3 Confirm whether customer action is required
3.4 Record that the communication was sent
```

### Step 4: Handle Response

```text
4.1 Answer clarifying questions
4.2 Escalate if the customer disputes the update
4.3 Reconfirm revised ETA or next milestone if anything changes
4.4 Close the loop after the action is completed
```

## Communication Rules

| Rule | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| Be accurate | Never share unverified timelines |
| Be proactive | Update before the customer has to chase |
| Be specific | Include next step and expected time |
| Be documented | Log every important communication |
| Be channel-aware | Use phone or WhatsApp for urgent cases, email for formal records |

## Escalation Triggers

- Customer disputes facts or timeline
- Delay creates SLA breach risk
- Damage, payment, or compliance issue is involved
- Customer requests supervisor or human manager
